My First Chapter of ScholarX '20

This article is a self write-up about my experience from the first month of being in the Class of 2020 of SEF ScholarX. It contains resources I have collected and also a record of my timeline, which I hope will be useful to you for your higher studies applications. Enjoy!

May 29th, 2020

First of all, I would like to thank SEF for this great opportunity they have given me and my class of 2020. I believe that they are doing a really wonderful endeavor by helping the local students in our country get connected with internationally acclaimed industrial and academic personnel. There efforts are truly helping to convert the long existing Brain Drain of Sri Lanka into a Brain Gain, of which I am very supportive to be a part of and will continue to help towards this endeavor.

To begin the first chapter of my ScholarX journey, I contacted my mentor Ruwandi Kariyawasam, Ph.D. Candidate at University of Toronto for the first time on April 27th. Before sending her my first email, I went on the internet to search about her research profile like a responsible freshly selected mentee.

These were the main articles I found and referred about my mentor, who was a researcher in the field of tropical infectious diseases with a background in evolutionary genetics.

  • https://ims.utoronto.ca/ruwandi-kariyawasam/
  • https://www.linkedin.com/in/ruwandikariyawasam/?originalSubdomain=ca
  • https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Ruwandi_Kariyawasam

I found this to be really fascinating and given that the world was also facing the Covid-19 pandemic during this period. This was an opportunity for me to tell about my interest in artificial intelligence and share this article I recently read https://diginomica.com/how-canadian-ai-start-bluedot-spotted-coronavirus-anyone-else-had-clue about how AI is being used together with infectious diseases.

We had our first google meets meeting for Scholar X Class of 2020 on May 1st , where we got to meet and get advice from Akshika, Saumiyaa and Minuri, the main organizers behind the ScholarX 2020 program. Minuri gave us this neat tool, to schedule our meetings with our mentors affectively, regardless of the time zone differences.

So I reached out to Ruwandi by email by introducing myself and a bit about my background from the last 4 years. After we exchanged introductions and the mandatory ScholarX 2020 guidelines via email, I was wondering if I should send her my CV. Since at our first ScholarX meeting, Saumiyaa had mentioned that our mentors were not provided with our CVs, Â I thought it would be good to send her my CV. In reply to the email I sent with my CV, Ruwandi replied with a suggestion to arrange our first meeting on the 14th of May. I was at home during the Lockdown, and I was very enthusiastic to meet her, and agreed for the meeting. She suggested that we meet around 8.30 am in SL time, and I thought I would go ahead and use that tool Minuri gave us. This is what the meeting looked like.

So, I prepared my questions and waited till the date of our first meeting. Even though I was not used to waking up very early the past few weeks, I made sure to wake up at 7.45 am on Thursday, 14th of May. I got up and freshened up and logged on to Skype and tried to find her Skype ID. It was around 8.30 am now, and I found her on skype and sent her message saying that I was online. However, she did not respond for a while, and I received a message from another person saying â€œHi, Iâ€™m sorry late for being lateâ€ and it happened to be Ruwandi on a different account. From the first moment we got to know each other, she was really nice and helpful in person as she was on email. I felt like I was very happy to have chosen her as my mentor and mostly to have a mentor who was a Canadian.

Our meeting went on for around 45 minutes meeting, which was around 11.45 pm from her time. I was very grateful that she would set aside so much time for me, that late at night. She was also kind enough to send me some really useful links for the things we discussed during the meeting as well. Some of the things we discussed during that meeting are listed below.

  • How is Canada a potential higher studies venue for me and details about Graduate Studies in Canada - https://www.educanada.ca/programs-programmes/graduate-studies-etudes-superieures.aspx?lang=eng
  • How to go through the research funding and awards opportunities for doing higher studies in Canada - https://www.canada.ca/en/services/science/researchfunding.html
  • Also discussed about researching the start-ups in Toronto related to Biomedical Sector - MaRS Discovery District (BlueDot is actually located here): https://www.marsdd.com/
  • Go through opportunities in UBC Toronto McMasters for Engineering PhDs https://www.bme.ubc.ca/ https://ibbme.utoronto.ca/
  • About writing my first blog post about the ScholarX program and what we learned and shared, as well as making my website.

She mentioned that my CV was really good, and that I would have a good chance of getting into a nice program and mentioned some things I could improve on. I noted down her suggestions for everything and also decided that I would convert my CV into an academic CV for future use as well.

We decided to have another meeting on May 28th, after I researched all of the information I found on the above matters. However, she had informed me later that week that we will have to reschedule our next meeting, since she will be defending her Ph.D. thesis in the middle of June. I wished her all the very best with her defense and told her that I will be well prepared for the next meeting with her, once her thesis defense was concluded. So, I have been spending my time doing some research on these topics that past few weeks and well as going to be spending the upcoming weeks on this matter as well.
